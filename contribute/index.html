Go Beyond Yourself: How to Contribute to Your Club?

🛠️

Meeting Setup and Cleanup

Help create a welcoming environment by setting up and cleaning up the meeting space

💡 Impact: Create a warm, professional meeting environment

Specific Actions:

  • •Arrive early to set up the meeting room and equipment
  • •Assist with preparing materials and technology
  • •Help clean up and organize after the meeting
  • •Coordinate meeting supplies and inventory management
🎭

Take on Meeting Roles

Volunteer for various meeting roles to develop skills and help drive meeting quality

💡 Impact: Improve meeting quality while developing your own skills

Specific Actions:

  • •Volunteer for meeting roles (Toastmaster, Evaluator, Timer, etc.)
  • •Carefully prepare and practice your role responsibilities
  • •Provide high-quality service during the meeting
  • •Help new members learn and succeed in their roles
🤝

Invite New Members

Help grow our community by inviting others to join Toastmasters

💡 Impact: Expand the club and build meaningful friendships

Specific Actions:

  • •Share Toastmasters' benefits with friends and colleagues
  • •Invite guests to attend club meetings
  • •Help new members feel welcome during their first visit
  • •Serve as a mentor to guide new members through their journey
🎉

Actively Participate in Social Activities

Strengthen club culture through social connections and team building

💡 Impact: Build a strong, supportive community culture

Specific Actions:

  • •Attend and participate in club social events and celebrations
  • •Help organize team-building activities and gatherings
  • •Contribute to holiday celebrations and special occasions
  • •Build genuine friendships with fellow members

Executive Committee Overview

👑

President

3-5 hours per week

The visionary leader of the club, responsible for strategic direction and overall operations

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Develop and execute club development strategy
  • •Chair executive team meetings and drive initiatives
  • •Represent the club externally and maintain relationships
  • •Ensure the club meets its mission and goals

Skills Developed:

Strategic leadership skillsExecutive decision-makingPublic relations and diplomacyOrganization managementVision-setting ability
📚

Vice President Education (VPE)

2-4 hours per week

Champion of member learning and development, overseeing the educational experience

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Arrange and schedule meeting speakers and roles
  • •Track and support member Pathways progress
  • •Provide educational resources and guidance
  • •Organize workshops and educational activities

Skills Developed:

Education and training expertiseProject management skillsHuman resource developmentCoaching and mentoring abilityOrganizational leadership
🎯

Vice President Membership (VPM)

2-3 hours per week

Growth and retention specialist, focused on welcoming and supporting members

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Develop and implement recruitment strategies
  • •Follow up with potential members and visitors
  • •Support current member satisfaction and retention
  • •Analyze membership trends and improve engagement

Skills Developed:

Marketing and promotion skillsRelationship building expertiseData analysis and insightsCustomer service excellenceStrategic thinking
📢

Vice President Public Relations (VPPR)

2-3 hours per week

Brand ambassador and communicator, managing club visibility and reputation

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Manage social media and online presence
  • •Create and distribute press releases and announcements
  • •Organize public relations activities and events
  • •Maintain and enhance club brand image

Skills Developed:

Marketing and communications skillsContent creation and writingMedia relations expertiseBrand managementPublic speaking ability
📝

Secretary

1-2 hours per week

Organized keeper of records and official communications for the club

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Record accurate meeting minutes and decisions
  • •Maintain member files and organizational records
  • •Handle official club communications
  • •Manage and organize club documents

Skills Developed:

Organization and detail managementCommunication coordinationInformation managementAdministrative expertiseAttention to detail
💰

Treasurer

1-2 hours per week

Financial steward, ensuring fiscal responsibility and transparency

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Manage club funds and financial accounts
  • •Prepare financial reports and statements
  • •Collect membership fees and handle payments
  • •Plan and manage club budgets

Skills Developed:

Financial management skillsBudget planning and controlData analysisFiscal responsibilityFinancial planning expertise
🔧

Sergeant at Arms (SAA)

1-2 hours per week

Meeting facilitator and technical support, ensuring smooth meeting operations

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Manage and maintain meeting equipment and technology
  • •Maintain meeting order and logistics
  • •Assist with overall meeting process flow
  • •Handle emergencies and unexpected situations

Skills Developed:

Technical skillsProblem-solving abilityService orientationEmergency response skillsTeam coordination
🎓

Immediate Past President (IPP)

2-3 hours per month

Wise guide and mentor, providing continuity and institutional knowledge

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Share past experiences and lessons learned
  • •Provide strategic consultation to current leadership
  • •Assist with major club decisions and transitions
  • •Guide and mentor new executive members

Skills Developed:

Mentoring and coaching skillsAdvisory leadershipLegacy and continuity buildingKnowledge transferLeadership wisdom

Why Join the Executive Committee?

📈

Enhance Leadership and Management Skills

Practice and develop essential leadership abilities in real-world situations through hands-on experience

🔍

Gain Deep Understanding of Club Operations

Understand organizational management and processes from an insider's perspective, building valuable operational knowledge

🌟

Make a Meaningful Impact

Directly shape club direction and drive positive change, seeing the results of your leadership and contributions

🏆

Earn International Recognition

Receive official Toastmasters certifications and awards acknowledging your leadership contribution and service

How to Start Contributing?

🌱

Start Small

Start with simple contributions, such as helping with meeting preparation and taking on basic roles

  • • Volunteer for meeting roles
  • • Help new members adapt
  • • Participate in club activities
📚

Learn and Observe

Observe how current executives work and learn from their leadership and management experience

  • • Attend executive meetings (as observer)
  • • Connect with current executives
  • • Understand each role's responsibilities
🚀

Take the Challenge

When ready, courageously run for an executive position to contribute to club development

  • • Prepare your election speech
  • • Develop your action plan
  • • Gain member support
