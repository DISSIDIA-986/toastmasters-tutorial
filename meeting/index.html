Meeting Process and Roles

Learn about Toastmasters meeting agenda and various role responsibilities.

Typical Meeting Timeline

Most Toastmasters meetings follow a structured 60-80 minute format. Here's what happens during a typical meeting:

6:10 - 6:15 PM
Meet & Greet + Introductions
6:15 - 6:25 PM
Opening + Meeting Support Roles Introduction
6:25 - 7:01 PM
Prepared Speeches (typically 3 speakers)
7:01 - 7:09 PM
Table Topics (Impromptu Speaking)
7:09 - 7:20 PM
Evaluations & Support Roles Reports
7:20 - 7:30 PM
Announcements, Recognition & Adjournment

Meeting Structure & Flow

🎯
Step 1

Opening & Introductions

Meet and greet, round table introductions, and introduction of meeting support roles (Timer, Grammarian, Ah-Counter)

→
📝
Step 2

Prepared Speeches

Typically 3 speakers deliver 5-7 minute speeches based on their Pathways projects, with evaluators providing feedback after each speech

→
💡
Step 3

Table Topics

Impromptu speaking practice where the Table Topics Master poses interesting questions and members respond on the spot (1-2 minutes each)

→
💬
Step 4

Evaluations & Reports

Table Topics Evaluator provides feedback, General Evaluator summarizes the meeting, and support roles (Timer, Grammarian, Ah-Counter) share their observations

→
🏆
Step 5

Closing

Guest comments, announcements, recognition of achievements, and meeting adjournment

Key Meeting Roles Explained

👑

Toastmaster of the Meeting

The meeting's host and conductor. Welcomes attendees, introduces speakers and their projects, maintains the meeting schedule, and ensures smooth transitions between segments.

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Welcome attendees and set the meeting tone
  • •Introduce each speaker and their Pathways project
  • •Keep the meeting flowing on schedule
  • •Fill time gaps and maintain positive energy

Skills Developed:

Leadership skillsHosting and presentation abilitiesConfidence buildingEvent management
🎤

Prepared Speaker

Delivers a 5-7 minute speech based on your Pathways project, applying specific speaking techniques and seeking feedback from evaluators.

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Prepare and practice your speech thoroughly
  • •Deliver your presentation with confidence
  • •Apply techniques from your Pathways project
  • •Accept feedback graciously and implement suggestions

Skills Developed:

Overcome fear of public speakingDevelop presentation skillsBuild confidenceApply leadership communication techniques
💭

Table Topics Master

Leads the impromptu speaking session by asking thought-provoking questions that members answer on the spot, creating a fun and supportive atmosphere.

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Prepare interesting and thought-provoking topics
  • •Invite participants to speak spontaneously
  • •Keep responses within 1-2 minute time limits
  • •Create an encouraging and positive atmosphere

Skills Developed:

Creative thinkingQuick thinking and adaptabilityLeadership in group dynamicsFacilitation skills
📊

Evaluator (Speech Evaluator)

Provides constructive feedback on a speaker's presentation, highlighting strengths and offering specific suggestions for improvement in a 2-3 minute evaluation.

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Listen carefully to the assigned speech
  • •Note specific strengths and areas for improvement
  • •Deliver a structured 2-3 minute evaluation
  • •Use the Commend-Recommend-Commend structure

Skills Developed:

Critical thinkingCommunication skillsConstructive feedback deliveryMentoring ability
�

Table Topics Evaluator

Provides feedback on impromptu speakers' Table Topics responses, recognizing their quick thinking and areas for improvement.

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Observe Table Topics responses carefully
  • •Provide feedback on impromptu speaking
  • •Recognize strong quick-thinking moments
  • •Offer constructive suggestions

Skills Developed:

Observation skillsFeedback expertiseRecognition of strengthsMentoring abilities
🎯

General Evaluator

Oversees the evaluation team and provides feedback on the entire meeting, including meeting flow, logistics, and overall effectiveness.

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Evaluate the evaluators' performances
  • •Comment on meeting flow and organization
  • •Note what went well and improvement areas
  • •Lead and coordinate the evaluation team

Skills Developed:

Strategic thinkingLeadership and team coordinationComprehensive analysis skillsBig-picture perspective
⏱️

Timer

Tracks time for each speaker and role, providing visual signals (green, yellow, red) to help speakers stay within time limits and manage their presentations effectively.

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Time all speakers and reports accurately
  • •Display timing signals at appropriate intervals
  • •Provide a comprehensive timing report
  • •Help speakers develop time management skills

Skills Developed:

Time management masteryAttention to detailResponsibility and accountabilityPrecision in execution
👂

Ah-Counter

Counts filler words and sounds (um, ah, like, you know) and unnecessary repetitions, helping speakers become aware of and eliminate speech habits.

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Track filler words and unnecessary sounds
  • •Note specific speakers and frequency
  • •Provide a detailed report with examples
  • •Help speakers develop cleaner speech patterns

Skills Developed:

Linguistic awarenessObservation skillsSpeech pattern recognitionSupportive coaching
�

Grammarian

Introduces a 'Word of the Day,' tracks its usage throughout the meeting, and notes excellent language use or grammatical improvements.

Main Responsibilities:

  • •Select and introduce an interesting Word of the Day
  • •Track and note usage of the word
  • •Recognize excellent vocabulary and phrasing
  • •Comment on grammar and language improvements

Skills Developed:

Language masteryVocabulary expansionAttention to detailTeaching and mentoring

Tips for Success in Your Roles

📚Before Your First Role

  • ✓Read the role instructions and responsibilities carefully
  • ✓Watch experienced members perform the role
  • ✓Ask questions and seek guidance from club leaders
  • ✓Prepare materials or forms in advance

🎯During Your Role

  • ✓Focus on your specific responsibilities
  • ✓Be enthusiastic and supportive
  • ✓Communicate clearly and confidently
  • ✓Remember that everyone is learning too

💬Giving Evaluations

  • ✓Commend: Start with what the speaker did well
  • ✓Recommend: Offer 1-2 specific suggestions
  • ✓Commend: End on a positive note
  • ✓Be specific, kind, and constructive

🚀Continuous Improvement

  • ✓Seek feedback from other members
  • ✓Try different roles to develop diverse skills
  • ✓Participate regularly to build confidence
  • ✓Remember: Progress, not perfection!

The "TAG Team" - Meeting Support Roles

The Timer, Ah-Counter, and Grammarian roles form the "TAG Team" - important support roles that help all members develop specific speaking and communication skills.

⏱️

Timer

Helps members develop time management skills by providing visual signals

  • 🟢 Green: Speak freely
  • 🟡 Yellow: 1 min left
  • 🔴 Red: Time's up
👂

Ah-Counter

Tracks filler words (um, ah, like) to help speakers develop cleaner speech patterns

Helps eliminate verbal crutches and fillers

📖

Grammarian

Introduces a "Word of the Day" and recognizes excellent language use

Enhances vocabulary and grammatical awareness
